The expert distro : Arch Linux

Arch Linux adheres to five principles: simplicity, modernity, pragmatism, user centrality and versatility. In general, the principles maintain minimal distribution-specific changes, minimal breakage with updates, pragmatic over ideological design choices.

Arch Linux uses a rolling release model, meaning there are no "major releases" of completely new versions of the system; a regular system update is all that is needed to obtain the latest Arch software; the installation images released by the Arch team are simply up-to-date snapshots of the main system components.
Arch Linux has comprehensive documentation, which consists of a community wiki known as the ArchWiki.

Personally, this is the distro I use. I find it very good for learning more about Linux. The rolling release philosophy also avoids some compatibility issues on recent hardware, especially with nvidia. However, I don't recommend it to novices.

The novice distro : Solus OS

Solus is an operating system that is designed for home computing. Every tweak enables us to deliver a cohesive computing experience.

Solus provides a multitude of experiences that enable you to get the most out of your hardware. From our flagship Budgie experience for modern devices to the more traditional MATE experience for lower-end devices, Solus aims to provide the best experience for your device.

Solus ships with a variety of software out of the box so you can get going without a lot of setup fuss. See our selection of default applications below!

From my point of view, solus is a modern and attractive distribution for all new linux users. The community is very friendly and it's easy to find support on their forum. Moreover, it is a distribution that features all the new technologies and keeps it up to date. I used it for a long time and I still highly recommend it.

The development distro : Pop!_OS

Pop!_OS is a Linux distribution, based on Ubuntu and using the GNOME Desktop Environment. It is intended for use by "developers, makers, and computer science professionals". Pop!_OS provides full disk encryption by default as well as streamlined window management, workspaces, and keyboard shortcuts for navigation.

Compatibility is something that most distributions don’t have a huge problem with, but Pop!_OS takes it to the next level. They two different Pop!_OS downloads: one for Intel/AMD drivers and one for Nvidia.

I had the opportunity to test this distro to resolve compatibility issues with my nvidia optimus card. And it turns out that Pop!_OS is probably the simplest solution to fix this common problem on Linux systems. Moreover, as Ubuntu based, it offers a wide range of packages which greatly simplifies its use and documentation.

The customizable distro : KDE Neon

The latest and greatest of KDE community software packaged on a rock-solid base.

KDE neon is the intersection of these needs using a stable Ubuntu long-term release as its core, packaging the hottest software fresh from the KDE Community ovens. Compute knowing you have a solid foundation and enjoy the features you experience in the world's most customisable desktop.

You should use KDE neon if you want the latest and greatest from the KDE community but the safety and stability of a Long Term Support release. When you don't want to worry about strange core mechanics and just get things done with the latest features. When you want your computer as your tool, something that belongs to you, that you can trust and that delivers day after day, week after week, year after year. Here it is: now get stuff done.

I haven't used KDE Neon personally but I've heard nothing but good feedback. It is a reliable and efficient distribution. It allows you to take full advantage of KDE which is highly configurable. I really like this distribution which can be used as a transition to more sophisticated distros such as Arch or Gentoo.
